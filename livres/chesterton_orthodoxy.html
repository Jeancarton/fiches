ORTHODOXY

Gilbert Keith Chesterton, 1908

Résumé

Ecouté en anglais en podcast gratuit.

Essai qui fait suite à son écrit précédent Heretics. En quoi pouvons-nous croire ?

Chapter 1
I did not make my philosophy. God and nature made my philosophy, and it made me.
He wanted, as every boy, to be in advance to the truth (by ten minutes) he realized he was 800 years late.
He is the fool of this story. Not his readers.

Chapter 2
If not confidence in ourselves, in what can we have confidence ?
The mad man is not the one who has lost his reason. He is the one who has lost everything except the reason.
Shakespeare was ok, it is his commentators who made him mad.
Eloge de la poésie. La source de la folie est dans la logique.
Nothing can save him but hunger for normality.
Against determinism. Free not to believe but, contrary to modern agnostic, free to believe too. Mysticism is necessary. If not : morbidity.
Le cercle a sa taille figée, la croix, parce qu’elle contient un paradoxe en elle, peut grandir et se développer, par la contradiction notamment.

Chapter 3
Virtues have been abandoned and are wandering alone. For example pity.
Dislocation of humility.
Doubtfull against himself but undoubtfull towards the truth. It has been reversed.
Attaquer la religion c’est attaquer la police sans savoir qu’il existe des voleurs
La sagesse n’est pas de donner des réponses mais de comprendre qu’il y a des énigmes.
Nietzsche pense la volonté comme un phénomène qui se répand, qui s’étend. Mais la volonté est toujours un acte d’auto-limitation par le choix.
Idem les anarchistes. Ne faisons pas les démagogues à demander aux triangles de s’échapper de leurs trois côtés.
Pour faire une satire, il faut reconnaître une norme dont l’autre s’écarte ? Comment Nietzsche alors peut-il être aussi satirique ?

Chapter 4
Principle in democracy : what is shared is more valuable than what is extraordinary.
Doesn’t agree with the fact that democracy is opposed to tradition. Democracy asks us not to neglect a good man ´s opinion even if he is our son.
Because of instinct of sex we like romantic stories. Because of instinct of astonishment we like fairy tales. Except babies because all is new for them.
Fairy philosophy.
Children are strong enough to survive monotony : « do it again! ». Grown up aren’t. May be God is.

Chapter 5
Defense of fanatic optimism. Christianity is a kind of fanatic optimism.

Chapter 6
The problem with the world is not that it is irregular or regular, but that it is almost regular. As if you look our members, they are symmetrical, but if you deduce a law, you will make a mistake with the heart. Christianity has the sense of regularity except for tiny final details.
Very hard for a man to defend something of which he is completely convinced.
Ce qui a surpris Chesterton dans son histoire, c’est que le christianisme était attaqué de toutes parts, mais par des arguments opposés entre eux : monde horrible d’ascèse ou paradis. Accuser le christianisme pour se battre trop peu, et en même temps pour avoir causé tant de guerres dans l’histoire…

Chapter 7
About evolution and progress.
There is no principle in nature. There is no equality nor inequality. Just a question of standard. Nature does not say cats are more valuable than mice.
We always speak in metaphors. Reform implies form : change the world in a particular form. When we want to change the world, we must think of whether we would want to change the world we would have created. Too quiet to think of liberty. Fed by revolutionary literature.
Progress must be fixed. It is not directed.
How can you make a man always dissatisfied with his work but always satisfied with working.
Nature is not our mother but our sister for christians.
Progress must be fixed and composed.
Ancient voice saying : « I could have said it to you a long time ago. » If you leave a thing alone it will go worse (against conservatives) : you must be always having a revolution.
The man should rule who does not think he can rule.
Also about aristocracy.
Stop social paradises and utopias.

Chapter 8
Profound laziness in our epoque.
Proposition to bring freedom in the church is a proposition to bring tyranny in the world.
Holiday : liberty of man. Miracle : liberty of God.
Difference between buddhism and christianity.
A buddhist saint has eyes closed, whereas a christian saint has wide open eyes.
To a christian existence is a story, that will end, not a science (like the Buddhists).
If you have no personal existence after death, can you have an existence now? Argument against any education.

Chapter 9
Why can’t you take only what you want in christianism? He will answer the question.
Human sacrifice is not so something old, but something new.
Christianity is a supernatural paradox.
Refutes the idea that christianity belongs to the dark ages. The church is the only thing which brought us out of it.
All living christians are dead pagans walking about.
Case for christianity is rational but not simple.
There can be not adventure in the land of anarchy, but many in the land of authority.
Whatever I am, I am not myself : original sin.
Both ancients (before christians) and moderns are miserables about existence.

Réflexions sur le livre (préciser la date)

2020-03 : Beaucoup de paradoxes, pour contredire ses adversaires. La part belle est donnée aux histoires, aux rêves (contre le rationalisme par exemple).
