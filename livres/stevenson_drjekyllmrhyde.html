THE STRANGE CASE OF DR. JEKYLL AND MR. HYDE

Robert Louis Stevenson, 1886

Résumé

Edward Hyde and doctor Heny Jekyll.
With a drug, Jekyll separates his wicked person, a double of himself, less tall, less old, because he has lived less.
Very interesting to have the dissociated personality and Jekyll's torments, but the use of a drug spoils the psychological analysis, I think.
