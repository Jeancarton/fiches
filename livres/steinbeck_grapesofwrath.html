THE GRAPES OF WRATH

John Steinbeck, 1939

Résumé

Tom Joad is out of jail after four years. Once, drunk, he had killed a man with a spade. He and former preacher Casy (he is no longer a preacher, as he always says) will go with the Joad family (Grandpa, Grandma, Uncle John, Pa, Ma, Rose of Sharon and Connie, Noah, Alfred, Ruthie and Winfield) to look for work in California, after being expropriated from their own land by the bank.
With millions of people, they are on the roads and in California, desperately looking for work, without enough to feed themselves. Living decently without anything, trying not to let the family fall apart (central position of Ma), leaded by the unofficial leaders : Ma and Tom (it should have been Pa or Uncle John).
Until Casy is brutally assassinated by cops, and revenged by Tom. Tom will flee and fight in the dark (see below the quote) defending human life and freedom against this revolting and deceitful californian organization.
"Majestic in its scale and moral vision; an eloquent tribute to the endurance and dignity of the human spirit".
